## ğŸ¯ What is This?

The Federal Grant Compliance Framework is a **free, open-source resource** that helps nonprofit organizations and research institutes manage federal grant compliance requirements. Everything is free to download, use, and adapt.

### The Problem

- Federal agencies distribute **$1.2 trillion** in grants annually
- **14,189 compliance findings** were identified in Single Audits (2022-2024)
- Commercial grant management software costs **$10,000-$100,000+** per year
- Consulting services cost **$150-$500** per hour
- Small nonprofits face the same compliance requirements but can't afford these solutions

### Our Solution

A comprehensive, validated framework based on implementation science research. Download the templates, policies, and tools you need. Use them immediately. Adapt them to your organization. All free under Creative Commons license.

## ğŸ“š What's Included

The framework covers **7 critical compliance domains**:

### 1. Financial Management & Reporting
- Grant portfolio tracking system
- SEFA (Schedule of Expenditures) builder
- Budget monitoring tools
- Cost allocation methodology
- Cash management procedures

### 2. Compliance Policies & Procedures
- Uniform Guidance compliance policy (2 CFR 200)
- Allowable cost policy with decision tools
- Procurement policy templates
- Time & effort reporting policies
- Travel, conflict of interest, and records retention policies

### 3. Eligibility Verification & Program Management
- Eligibility determination framework
- Participant file management system
- Service delivery documentation templates

### 4. Reporting & Performance Management
- Reporting calendar with deadline tracking
- Federal Financial Report (FFR/SF-425) preparation guide
- Performance report templates
- Prior approval request tracking

### 5. Subrecipient Monitoring
- Subrecipient vs. contractor determination tool
- Risk assessment instrument
- Monitoring plan templates
- Subaward agreement templates
- FFATA/FSRS reporting guide

### 6. Audit Readiness & Internal Controls
- Single Audit preparation guide
- Internal controls framework (COSO-based)
- Segregation of duties matrix
- Grant file organization system
- Closeout procedures checklist

### 7. Training & Capacity Building
- Role-based training curricula (executive, grants administrator, finance staff, program staff)
- New employee onboarding materials
- Reference library with annotated Uniform Guidance
- Quick-reference guides and checklists

**Total: 120+ templates, tools, checklists, and guides**

### Not Legal Advice
This framework provides information and tools based on federal regulations (2 CFR 200 Uniform Guidance) and compliance best practices. It is **not legal advice**. Organizations should:
- Consult with qualified legal counsel for specific situations
- Verify current regulations (requirements change frequently)

We strive for accuracy, but:
- Federal regulations change periodically

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this really free?**  
A: Yes. Completely free under Creative Commons license. No costs, no registration required.

**Q: Do I need special software?**  
A: No. Templates work with Microsoft Excel/Word or free alternatives like Google Sheets/Docs.

**Q: How do I know it's accurate?**  
A: Every requirement is traced to 2 CFR 200 Uniform Guidance or specific agency guidance. The framework has been reviewed by grants management professionals, auditors, and federal program officers.

**Q: What if my federal agency has special requirements?**  
A: The framework covers core Uniform Guidance requirements applicable to all federal grants. You'll need to layer agency-specific requirements on top. We provide guidance on how to identify and incorporate these.

**Q: Can I modify the templates?**  
A: Yes! The CC BY 4.0 license explicitly permits adaptation. Customize everything to fit your organization's needs.

**Q: Can consultants use this with clients?**  
A: Absolutely. The license permits commercial use. We encourage consultants to contribute improvements back to benefit the community.

**Q: What if I find an error?**  
A: Please open an issue on GitHub or email damoah.dao@gmail.com. We review and correct errors promptly.

See [docs/FAQ.md](docs/FAQ.md) for more questions.
